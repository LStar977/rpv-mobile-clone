## Page 1 — The voice between elections

You vote once every four years. They decide everything else.

Between elections — the years when most decisions actually get made — there's no real way to be heard. Your participation is reduced to social-media noise, anonymous petitions, or town halls where the loudest voice wins. Petitions can be faked. Polls can be brigaded. Comment sections are bots. Governments and institutions act on data nobody can trust, because nobody can prove who's behind it.

**Represent Vote turns real, verified voices into trusted consensus.**

Verified citizens vote on the issues that shape their communities — federal, provincial, municipal — in the months and years between formal elections. Every vote is anonymous from other voters but cryptographically tied to a real, unique adult. The result is the first medium where ordinary people can register a position on a public issue with the weight of a real vote — not the noise of a petition or a poll.

### What makes it different

- **One person, one verified ballot.** Identity verification by Didit. No bots, no duplicates, no manipulation.
- **Verified, not revealed.** Your ID is checked once and never published. You vote under a private identity. Your face and documents are deleted after 30 days.
- **Audit-grade results.** Every ballot is cryptographically signed. Every total is independently verifiable. No black boxes.
- **Yes/no, multi-choice, and ranked-choice voting.** Whatever the question requires.
- **Geographic scope.** Some questions affect everyone; some affect only your city. Every proposal is scoped to the right level.

### The Alberta moment

Right now, Alberta is debating its future with Canada. For the first time, residents can register a *verified* position on the separation question — not an anonymous poll, not a forgeable petition, but a real, identity-checked, one-person-one-ballot voice that becomes part of the public record.

The conversation that shapes the formal referendum is happening now. Represent Vote makes sure it counts.

## Page 2 — How it works

**Three steps to a verified voice.**

### 1. Verify

Confirm you're a real person with a government-issued ID. Two minutes. Free. Your identity is verified by Didit, our KYC partner — your passport or driver's license is scanned, your face is matched to it via a live selfie, and your region is extracted from the document. Your face and documents are deleted from our verification partner 30 days later; only the verification *decision* (verified yes/no, country/state/city) is retained.

For citizens-only ballots like the Alberta separation referendum, the stronger **Citizen verification** uses passport + proof of address (utility bill, bank statement, lease).

### 2. Vote

Swipe through proposals in your region — federal, provincial, and municipal. Cast your ballot in seconds.

- **Yes/no** — swipe left to oppose, right to support
- **Multiple-choice** — pick one of the offered options
- **Ranked-choice** — rank options in order of preference, tallied via instant-runoff voting (the same method used in real elections like Alaska, Maine, and New York City)

Your vote is recorded on a tamper-evident audit log. For yes/no votes, your participation is cryptographically attested on the Base blockchain as a public proof you voted — without revealing how.

### 3. See impact

Watch results update in real time. Every vote is cryptographically signed and the outcome is independently verifiable. Your civic record — every proposal you voted on, every issue you weighed in on — lives on your verified profile as a permanent, exportable record of participation.

### Why verification matters

Most "civic engagement" platforms don't verify identity. Petitions accept anyone with an email. Polls accept anyone with a click. The data they generate is suggestive at best and junk at worst — nobody in power treats it as binding.

Represent Vote verifies every voter against a government ID. When 10,000 verified Albertans say yes to a proposal, it carries the weight of 10,000 real people — not 10,000 email addresses someone bought.

That's the difference between a petition and a referendum. That's the difference between noise and signal.

## Page 5 — Why now, and what's next

### Why now

The traditional channels of civic participation are eroding. Voter turnout in non-election years is effectively zero. Even in election years, between 30 and 60 percent of eligible Canadians vote. Town halls are dominated by the loudest. Petitions are signed by bots. Polls miss the people who matter most.

Meanwhile, the most consequential decisions — pipelines, transit corridors, school closures, zoning, budget priorities, even the question of provincial separation — are made between elections, when the public has no real voice.

Represent Vote is the platform built for that gap. We launched in Alberta during the separation referendum moment because it's the clearest possible example of what we exist for: an entire province debating a question that won't formally appear on a ballot for years. The conversation is happening now. The decisions that shape the question are happening now. Verified citizens deserve a real way to weigh in.

### Where we go from here

- **2026:** Scale across Alberta on the referendum hook. Onboard first paying union, HOA, and school customers.
- **2027:** Expand to BC, Saskatchewan, Ontario. Begin municipal pilot programs.
- **2028:** National rollout. International expansion to Commonwealth countries with strong democratic infrastructure.
- **Long term:** Become the credible civic-input layer for any democracy that wants verified, audit-grade public consensus.
