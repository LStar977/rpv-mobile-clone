WHY THIS EXISTS

The biggest petition in Canadian history was dismissed in one news cycle.

In December 2023, 386,698 people signed a parliamentary e-petition — the largest in Canadian history. It changed nothing. Signatures only required an email address, so critics could wave it away as bots, duplicates, and foreign interference. Nobody could prove them wrong.

The moment a result matters, “it’s all fake” ends the conversation.

That’s the flaw in every petition, every online poll, every hashtag.

Represent makes that attack impossible. Every voter is verified against a government document. Every ballot is tied to a verified residence. Every tally can be audited by anyone — including the people who don’t like the result.

We count in public.

Why this counts when polls don't

One person, one ballot Every count is verified humans, each counted once. No bots, no brigades, no duplicate accounts — verification makes them impossible, not just against the rules.
Counted, not estimated A poll asks 1,000 people and models the rest. Represent counts every ballot actually cast and shows you the exact number — with the math open to audit.
Never for sale No ads. No selling reach, placement, or results. The record can't be bought — that's the entire point of keeping one.

“Why would I give a voting app my ID?”

We never see your documents the way you fear. Verification is handled by a certified identity provider. We receive a yes/no and your verified region — not copies of your passport. A breach of our servers cannot leak documents we never hold.
Your vote is pseudonymous in public. The public ledger shows that a verified citizen voted — not your name. Identity and ballot are linked only enough to prevent fraud, never for display.
We will never sell your data or run political ads. Ever. Our policy bans political advertising permanently. We make money from optional premium features and organization tools — not from you being the product.
