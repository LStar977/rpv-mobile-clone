WHY THIS EXISTS

The biggest petition in Canadian history was dismissed in one news cycle.

In December 2023, 386,698 people signed a parliamentary e-petition — the largest in Canadian history. It changed nothing. Signatures only required an email address, so critics could wave it away as bots, duplicates, and foreign interference. Nobody could prove them wrong.

The moment a result matters, “it’s all fake” ends the conversation.

That’s the flaw in every petition, every online poll, every hashtag.

Represent makes that attack impossible. Every voter is verified against a government document. Every ballot is tied to a verified residence. Every tally can be audited by anyone — including the people who don’t like the result.

PLAIN ANSWERS

Asked constantly. Answered honestly.

Is my vote anonymous?Ballots are tied to a public per-person address, not your name. Your name is never published; the record proves one person voted once without saying who they are.
What happens to my ID?It's checked to prove you're one real person, then discarded. We keep the proof of verification — never the document.
Who can see the results?Everyone. Every count is public at 25 or more verified ballots — no account required, and no one can pay to promote or bury a result.
Is this legally binding?No — and we won't pretend otherwise. Represent is the verified public record of what citizens actually want. Its power is that no official can say “nobody asked for this” when thousands of verified constituents are on the record.
