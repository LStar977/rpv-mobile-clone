---

## The one strategic idea everything hangs on

**Your marketing material is the tally itself.** No startup language, no "we're excited to
announce." The content is: *here is where verified Albertans stand, updated live, and on
October 19 we all find out together how close we were.* Every post is a number with a date
attached. That's why this can work without an ad budget — the referendum supplies the
urgency, you supply the only verified count.

**The recurring beat:** every Sunday until Oct 19, post the updated tally. Same format
every week. People follow series, not announcements.

## 8. The two rules that protect you

1. **Never advocate a side, anywhere, ever** — not even a winking emoji. The moment
   Represent looks like it wants a particular answer on Q10, the platform is dead. Your
   only rooting interest, stated openly: a big verified turnout.
2. **Paid ads about the referendum: check before you spend.** Alberta has third-party
   advertiser registration rules for referendum advertising. Organic posts about your
   product are fine; *paid* promotion that references the referendum may require
   registering with Elections Alberta. Ten minutes with their third-party advertising page
   (or one email to them) before you boost a single post. Unsexy, existential.
