Equity Terms

$5,000,000 Pre-money
$6,000,000 Post-money
20% Fully-funded

The investment of $1,000,000 is offered at a $5,000,000 pre-money valuation ($6,000,000 post-money) for 20% fully-funded.

Proposed structure — equity released pro-rata with capital: rather than transferring the full 20% on signing, shares are issued as each milestone funds (1% at signing, then 1%, 3%, 4%, 5%, and 6% across Milestones 1–5 — totalling 20% at full funding). This protects both sides: the investor never holds equity beyond capital actually invested, and the company never sells a fifth of itself against funds that benchmarks may not unlock. Alternative implementations with identical economics (milestone-priced share issuances, or warrants vesting per milestone) are acceptable; legal counsel at signing papers whichever both parties prefer.

If the investor requires the full 20% on signing, the company requests a reciprocal commitment: milestones become obligations subject only to benchmark achievement (not discretionary), with an agreed remedy (equity clawback or buy-back at cost) for any milestone not funded after benchmarks are met.
