How the business works

The economics.

Acquiring verified users

We model a blended cost of $10–15 per verified user. Paid acquisition runs $20–25; the referendum’s organic reach, partner-led votes, creator distribution, and a no-install browser flow pull the blend well below that. The October 19 referendum is the unpaid acquisition engine — paid spend supplements it, it does not carry it.

How the $1,000,000 reaches 50,000 verified users
User acquisition & campaign distribution~$600,000
Founder execution & operations~$270,000
Product, legal & investor readiness~$130,000
How Represent earns
–Organizations — unions, associations, and municipalities pay for governance & voting tools ($5K–$50K annual contracts). The near-term revenue engine.
–Government & institutional — verified public-consultation tooling, sold as contracts.
–Consumer premium — power features (analytics, monitoring) at $5/mo for engaged users.

Milestone 5’s $10,000+ MRR target is the proof that at least one of these lines converts at scale.

Risks & defensibility

What could go wrong — and why we win anyway.

Risks & mitigations
Referendum delayed or cancelled
The platform is not referendum-dependent. The verified network and the organization/government revenue paths continue; targets shift to the same benchmarks on a later timeline.
Verification conversion below model
User benchmarks carry a floor and one 30-day extension. The no-install browser flow already removes the largest drop-off, and the paid/organic mix re-weights to whatever converts.
Regulatory & advertising compliance
Elections Alberta third-party advertising rules are assessed and cleared before any paid referendum advertising. The platform is explicitly independent and non-binding.
Single-channel dependency
App, browser flow, and organization tools are already shipped — the company is not betting on one channel or one feature.
Why Represent wins
–Verification is the moat. Every user is a real, ID-checked human. Competitors built on email sign-ups cannot match the credibility — or fake the count.
–First mover on verified civic at a real, dated, high-salience event.
–Network effects. The verified-citizen graph compounds — the next vote happens where the verified voters already are.

Equity Terms

$5,000,000 Pre-money
$6,000,000 Post-money
20% Fully-funded

The investment of $1,000,000 is offered at a $5,000,000 pre-money valuation ($6,000,000 post-money) for 20% fully-funded.

The $5,000,000 pre-money reflects a live, App Store-approved product — verification, browser voting, and organization tools already shipped — a once-in-a-decade acquisition catalyst in the October 19 referendum, and a revenue path through organizations and government beyond consumer subscriptions. It is set to leave clear headroom for a step-up at the Seed round.

Proposed structure — equity released pro-rata with capital: rather than transferring the full 20% on signing, shares are issued as each milestone funds (1% at signing, then 1%, 3%, 4%, 5%, and 6% across Milestones 1–5 — totalling 20% at full funding). This protects both sides: the investor never holds equity beyond capital actually invested, and the company never sells a fifth of itself against funds that benchmarks may not unlock. Alternative implementations with identical economics (milestone-priced share issuances, or warrants vesting per milestone) are acceptable; Final structure and documentation should be prepared by legal counsel in the form both parties agree is most appropriate.

If the investor requires the full 20% on signing, the company requests a reciprocal commitment: milestones become obligations subject only to benchmark achievement (not discretionary), with an agreed remedy (equity clawback or buy-back at cost) for any milestone not funded after benchmarks are met.

Toward the Seed Round.
