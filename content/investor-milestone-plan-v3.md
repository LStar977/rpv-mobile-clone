## Where the product is today

This is not a plan to build a product. The product is live.

- **iOS app approved and live on the App Store** — including identity verification,
  geo-gated voting, citizens-only ballots, discussion, and Apple-approved subscriptions
- **Browser voting live** — anyone can verify and vote from a shared link, no install
- **The Shadow Referendum is running now**: all 10 official questions from Alberta's
  October 19 referendum, verbatim ballot wording, open only to identity-verified
  Albertans; every vote recorded on a public audit trail
- **Identity pipeline operational** — government-ID + liveness verification (certified
  KYC provider), location taken from documents, not GPS
- **B2B layer built** — private organization voting (unions, associations, municipalities)
- **Total infrastructure cost: $125/month.** The founder builds and maintains everything.

What remains to prove is not the product — it's demand, at scale, on a deadline the
province has already set.

## The opportunity

Alberta votes in an official 10-question referendum on **October 19, 2026**. Every poll
about it shares one weakness: unverified respondents. Represent runs the only count
built entirely from identity-verified Albertans — and on October 19 it becomes the only
poll in the country checkable against an official result, in public, the same night.

That comparison is the credibility event this plan is built around. Capital is deployed
when attention is cheapest (the 18 weeks before the vote), the result is banked as the
company's flagship proof, and the remaining milestones sell that proof up the chain:
retained users → organizations → municipalities → Series Seed.

**Positioning (fixed, in all materials):** Represent is an independent civic platform.
The Shadow Referendum is an unofficial, non-binding public opinion measure. We never
advocate a side; our only rooting interest is verified turnout.

**Reserve Rules:** the performance reserve is not discretionary padding. It deploys only
into channels currently beating the verified-user CAC target for that milestone, is
reported monthly with channel attribution, and unspent balances roll into the next
milestone's reserve. The investor sees every deployment in the monthly report.

**Floors & Extensions:** M2 and M3 user benchmarks carry a defined floor (≈70% of
target) plus one 30-day extension. Hitting the floor within the extension releases the
tranche at the investor's option; missing the floor pauses the structure per the
protections below. Defining this now prevents a near-miss from becoming a dispute later.

## Risk Register

|---|---|
| Referendum delayed or cancelled | Campaign pivots to "Alberta Verified" standing civic network; targets shift to M3 timelines; the platform is not referendum-dependent — the referendum is an accelerant, not the business |

| Single-founder dependency | Documented codebase, AI-assisted development stack, investor board seat; key-person insurance can be added at M1 if required |
| Platform neutrality attacked | Hard rules: no political ads ever, both-sides creator contracts, official ballot wording verbatim, public audit trail — the defense is structural, not rhetorical |

## Equity Terms

$1,000,000 for **20% fully funded** at a $5,000,000 pre-money valuation — **equity
released pro-rata with capital**: 1% at T0, 1% at M1, 3% at M2, 4% at M3, 5% at M4,
6% at M5 (20% total at full funding).

This protects both sides: the investor never holds equity beyond capital actually
invested, and the company never sells a fifth of itself against tranches that
benchmarks may not unlock. Identical economics via tranche-priced issuances or
per-tranche warrants are acceptable; counsel papers the preferred form at T0.

If the investor requires the full 20% at signing, the company requests the reciprocal:
tranches become obligations subject only to benchmark achievement, with an agreed
equity clawback for any tranche unfunded after benchmarks are met.
